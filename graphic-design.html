Malaria No More

Print Collateral | NEXT

Select in-house print design for non-profit Malaria No More including print collateral for the organization's 2013 Benefit, and an informational brochure related to the AFCON (African Cup of Nations) tournament. The brochure recaps, for prospective supporters of the cause, how this media platform leveraged Africa's most popular sport to raise malaria awareness among men, women and children alike. The success of the campaign and campaign documentation helped secure malaria a spot as the official social cause of the next two AFCON tournaments in 2015 and 2017.

Mayors Against Illegal Guns Campaign

Digital | PREV | NEXT

Selects of social media collateral created for the Demand Action campaign from the Mayors Against Illegal Guns Coalition in early 2013. Given the seriousness and importance of the campaign, my main goal was to create graphics that properly honored the powerful statistics we were communicating, whilst remaining engaging enough to command the relatively short attention span of the average social media user.

The Princess and the Comb

Print / Bookbinding | PREV | NEXT

The Princess and the Comb is a two-tiered investigation into the concept of sustainability. The first tier is a bastardized fairy tale involving a magic kingdom struck down by over-consumption. This story literally unfolds into the second tier: a series of informational spreads that elaborate on certain key concepts from the fairy tale. Neither narrative is didactic; they seek only to explore the psychology and complexity of the sustainability, for instance, examining the emotional dimension of consumption, the human tendency towards ownership or the hypocrisy of technological literacy. Full explanation and process documentation can be found here.
