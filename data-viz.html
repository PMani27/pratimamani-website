Exoplanets

Digital | NEXT

An infographic based on data from The Exoplanet Data Explorer. The infographic displays the 50 closest exoplanets to Earth in terms of their distance from the earth in lightyears, the date of discovery of the exoplanetary system, the size of the exoplanetary orbit (adjusted using a logrithm conversion), and where they lie relative to the habitable zone around the star. Spectral classes of stars and orbital period/mass of the exoplanets are displayed on the edges of the graphic. Thanks to Anze Slosar from the Brookhaven National Laboratory, for guidance with the project.

US DEBT

Digital | PREV | NEXT

The US Debt figures are an obvious choice for data-visualization. However, with so many designers representing this data, I wanted to have a specific focus in my piece, which ended up being the aggregate effect from the foreign holders perspective, ie. showing which ten countries held the most US Debt in averaged cumulative terms over the past decade. Also featured are simple bar graphs showing the actual (not averaged) year-to-year amount held by each country. For example, we can see that Japan saw a sudden drop in how much debt it bought, whilst China bought steadily more over the decade.
