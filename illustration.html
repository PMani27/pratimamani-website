Introducing the Court

Pen on paper, digitally edited | NEXT

A personal project that shamelessly marries three nerdy interests: illustrative typography, medieval culture and characters. I pulled the trademark figures from your average myth/legend/fable and composed the silhouettes from words and phrases typical to each one. Introducing the court: the white knight, dark knight, king, old crone and damsel.

Smokers

Gouache, ink and colour pencil on paper | PREV | NEXT

Selects from an illustration series documenting my friends' stranger smoking rituals: Camille is cigarette-Frankenstein, piecing her smokes back together when they break. Marquette lets her empty cigarette packs pile up into towering skyscrapers about her bed, where she sits nestled in an electric pink bathrobe whist watching Netflix behind bug eyeglasses.

Gerald

Gouache on paper, digitally edited | PREV | NEXT

An entry for the Parsons New School stand at the 2010 Bologna Children's Illustration fair under the theme of the 'bestiary'. My invented creature is part rabbit, frog, wildebeest, elephant and dinosaur. His name is Gerald. He's shy.

ITL Wall

Paint | PREV | NEXT

International Theatre Lab (ITL) is an experimental theatre company situated in the Sunnmøre Alps region of Western Norway. The couple who run ITL were facing challenges attracting visitors to the theatre, and approached me to help out on the design front. Over the course of my one-month stay, I created posters, flyers and signage for ITL's weekly creative 'Open House', and a painting for the left wall of the building. Credit to Valentin Gadjokova, Petyr Popovic and Anna Possarnig for their contributions.
